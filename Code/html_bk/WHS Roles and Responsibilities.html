​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​In​trodu​ction

Everyone who works for or on behalf of Data#3 and visits our premises and work areas has responsibilities for work health and safety (WHS). 

From Data#3's Board of Directors to staff working in our offices and on our client sites, it is important that responsibilities are understood and personnel take action to fulfil their duties and responsibilities. This also includes contractors providing services to Data#3 and contractors, consultants, suppliers and visitors to our premises and work areas. 

Duty Holders under WHS Legislation

There are four main duty holders under WHS legislation - officers, PCBU (person conducting the business or undertaking, workers and others.  Definitions for these duty holders is shown in this diagram.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Roles and Responsibilities

The table below lists the WHS duties for the 4 duty holder in WHS legislation and some wider roles and responsibilities for health and safety at work.

​POSITION​​​

​RESPONSIBILITIES

Officer​

Officers exercise due diligence to ensure Data#3 meets its WHS duty of care and legal obligations as the person conducting the business or undertaking (PCBU)​

Data#3​

(the Organisation)

Primary Duty of Care:

  • Provide and maintain a safe and healthy work environment, safe plant and structures and safe systems of work
  • Safe use, handling and storage of plant, structures and substances
  • Provide adequate welfare facilities for work carrying out work and access to these facilities
  • Provide information, training, instruction of supervision to protect persons from risks to health and safety
  • Monitor the health of workers and conditions at the workplace t​o minimise illness or injury of workers​

Workers 

​

Workers are responsible for:
  • Taking care of their health and safety
  • Ensuring their actions do not affect the health and safety of others
  • Complying with reasonable WHS instructions, policies, procedures, signs and rules
  • Stopping work or calling a stop work if work is not safe to continue
  • Reporting concerns with health and safety, hazards or incidents to their Team Leader

Contractors​​/Sub-Contractors

Contractors/Sub-Contractors who supply services to Data#3 are considered a PCBU under WHS legislation and are responsible for fulfilling PCBU duties in the provision of services. In addition to meeting their duties as the PCBU, contractors are responsible for fulfilling their responsibilities as a Worker:
  • Taking care of their health and safety and ensuring their actions or inactions do not adversely affect the health and safety of themselves or others
  • Following reasonable WHS instructions, policies, procedures, signs and rules as provided by Data#3 and its clients
  • Supporting their Data#3 representative and others they work with in ensuring WHS requirements are met
  • Reporting concerns with health and safety, hazards or incidents to their Data#3 representative

Visitors

​

Visitors who attend a Data#3 client site, workplace, or premises are responsible for:
  • Taking care of their health and safety
  • Ensuring their actions or inactions do not adversely affect the health and safety of others​
  • Following reasonable WHS instructions, policies, procedures, signs and rules
  • Reporting concerns with health and safety, hazards or incidents to their Data#3 point of contact​

First Aiders

​

In addition to their duties as a Worker, First Aiders are responsible for:
  • Being suitably qualified and keeping first aid qualifications up-to-date
  • Providing first aid treatment within their level of competence
  • Ensuring first aid kits under their control are adequately stocked and equipment well maintained
  • Recording first aid treatment provided, however big or small
  • Keeping confidential personal/medical information received in the course of first aid duties
  • Reporting any deficiencies in first aid to their Team Leader or HSE

Chief Fire Warden / Deputy Fire Warden

​

In addition to their duties as a Worker, Chief Fire Wardens are responsible for:
  • Ensuring Fire and Evacuation Diagram and Plans are current and visible 
  • Ensuring fire/emergency equipment is installed and maintained where required
  • Monitoring for non-compliance with fire safety regulations and taking action to address these where required
  • Coordinating or participating in evacuation practices, holding post evacuation reviews and taking action to manage identified non-conformances and defects
  • Ensuring Personal Emergency Evacuation Plans (PEEPS) are available for personnel needing assistance in the event of an evacuation​
  • Providing or arranging for first response evacuation instruction and emergency/fire evacuation coordination instructions in the event of an emergency
  • Liaising with the attending fire and emergency services. emergency personnel and others as relevant if there is a fire or emergency on the premises
In the absence of the Chief Fire Warden, Deputy Wardens are responsible for fulfilling the role and responsibilities of the CFW.

Emergency Wardens

​

In addition to their duties as a Worker, Emergency Wardens are responsible for:​
  • Participating in emergency warden training
  • Maintaining an up-to-date knowledge of the premises/work area emergency diagrams and plans
  • Ensuring workers and others in their work area are familiar with emergency diagrams and procedures
  • During drills and evacuations, wear the designated identification (e.g. yellow hat or red hat)
  • In the event of an emergency:
    • Establishing the nature of emergency where possible
    • Ordering the evacuation where necessary
    • Following directions of the Chief Fire Warden and/or attending Emergency Services
    • Informing​ persons present in the workplace of the requirement to evacuate and verbally encouraging persons to evacuate the area while moving toward the designated exit (Note: Wardens are not expected to unnecessarily delay their own exit or jeopardize their own safety at any time).
    • Only if there is no immediate danger, ensuring windows and doors are closed en-route to exit in the event of a fire.
    • Escorting persons requiring assistance to the pre-designated safe area and notifying the Chief Fire Warden and/or attending emergency services of their location
    • Providing an accurate report to the Chief Fire Warden and/or attending Emergency Services
    • Following evacuation, waiting until the “all clear” before returning to the work area
  • Following a drill or emergency, reporting and discussing concerns with the Chief Fire Warden, Office Manager and/or HSE as relev​ant​​.

Rehabilitation and Return to Work Co-ordinators​

In addition to their duties as a Worker and in relation to WHS responsibilities, Rehabilitation and Return to Work Co-ordinators are responsible for:
  • Promoting early reporting and recording of injuries to enable early employee contact and (where required) reporting to Data#3's workers compensation insurer/s and WHS Regulator
  • Being aware of health and safety considerations for rehabilitation and return to work of personnel with injury or ill health and referring to HSE services for guidance where relevant to manage hazards and risks 
  • Maintaining confidential information received for the purposes of injury or ill health management, rehabilitation and return to work

Purchasing and Procuring Goods or Services​

In addition to their duties as a Worker, persons who purchase or procure goods and services for the Organisation are responsible for:
  • Ensuring WHS risks posed by goods or services are considered and managed through purchasing and procurement processes
  • Where WHS requirements are identified, these are specified and met by the supplier or contractor
  • Ensuring Data#3's WHS processes for sub-contractor management are followed and met
  • Ensuring all necessary HSE information is supplied with goods or services received, such as technical manuals, safety data sheets, information and compliance data
  • Referring to LARA and/or HSE Services where queries or concerns are raised or other guidance is required​
