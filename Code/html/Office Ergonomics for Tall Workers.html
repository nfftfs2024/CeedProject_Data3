Introduction

For the majority of office workers, adjustments to standard office equipment will enable a comfortable working posture. For tall workers however, standard office furniture may not provide for their increased height and size and consideration is needed to enable a comfortable work posture.

This Information Page provides information for workers who are tall and use a computer.  It should be read in conjunction with the Office Ergonomics page in the HSE System.

How High is Tall?

There is no specific height at which a person may be considered "tall", however standard office furniture is designed for people between the 5th and 95th percentile for a western male. The 95th percentile is 185cm or 6'1", so people about this height or taller may need alternative furniture or work station set up if they cannot achieve a comfortable work posture with standard office furniture.

Other considerations for workplace adjustments include the length of time a person will use their computer at their work station, opportunities to vary work practices and positions and whether they have musculo-skeletal symptoms that may be caused or aggravated by their posture and computer use.

Workstation Assessment

All Data#3 personnel using a computer must complete a  Workstation Self-Assessment Checklist, this includes workers who are tall. Where reasonable adjustments using the office equipment provided by Data#3 does not allow tall workers to have a comfortable work station set up, consideration should be given to providing alternative furniture and equipment suitable for tall people.

Office Ergonomics for Tall People

Considerations for tall people fall in 5 key areas; desk, chair, monitor, keyboard/mouse and interaction with equipment and task. Specific requirements will vary between people.

Desk

The optimal height for a desk for tall person is up to 33" (inches). Options for this are:

  • Height adjustable desk that can be raised to a height that suits the user.
  • Desk risers that go under the desk feet, to raise the height of the desk.
  • If a computer bench is used, raising the height to suit the tall computer user (ensuring other users are also comfortable).

A good check for the ensuring the desk height is suitable - when the tall worker is sitting or standing at their desk, their shoulders should be relaxed, forearms parallel with the desk and in a comfortable position.

Chair

Taller people should have a chair that enables them to sit and move comfortably at their desk, for tall people this may mean providing a chair suited to a taller person. Options for this are:

  • Replace the spoke and gas lift in a standard office chair with a longer spoke and gas lift. The chair supplier may be able to advise if this is possible.
  • Purchase a chair suited for taller people, which will have a heigher spoke, larger seat pan, higher and wider back rest and higher seat position.

The preference is to provide a chair designed for a taller person, rather than a taller person sitting in a lower less suitable chair.

Monitor

The monitor should be placed with the top of the screen approximately eye height, as shown on the Office Ergonomics page in the HSE System..

Keyboard and Mouse

Taller persons tend to have longer and larger arms and hands. A standard size keyboard and mouse may be comfortable, however for those spending a longer time using the keyboard or mouse (>2/hours day), it may be worth considering a larger ergonomic keyboard and mouse such as the Microsoft Surface Ergonomics Keyboard and the Microsoft Sculpt Ergonomic Desktop Wireless Keyboard, Number Pad and Mouse. Contact HSE if advise is needed for this.

Interaction with Equipment and Task

Once the work station and computer equipment are set up, the taller worker is responsible for ensuring these are adjusted to suit their size and posture and for ensuring they have a comfortable and ergonomic posture when sitting or standing to use their computer. The Workstation Self-Assessment Checklist should be completed once the work station and computer equipment are set up, to ensure a comfortable set up to suit the taller worker.

Musculo-Skeletal Symptoms

Taller workers should report any musculo-skeletal symptoms they experience when using their computer and working at their workstation. Musculo-skeletal symptoms are best treated promptly by identifying and restricting activities that may have caused or aggravate the symptoms, rest and, if needed, a treatment plan directed by their GP.

Where concerns are raised, the following actions should be taken:

  • Notify their Manager or Supervisor, complete a Workstation Self-Assessment Checklist and make improvements to their work area and posture, if necessary purchasing equipment suited to taller workers.
  • If symptoms are unresolved, worker should see their GP for an examination and advice on ways to resolve symptoms.
  • Refer to their HR Advisor and/or HSE by logging a BCS for the attention of WHS.

When HSE are notified, they may undertake an assessment to understand the cause of the symptoms and agree actions to address the concerns raised.
